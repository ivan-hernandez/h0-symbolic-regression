# P3: Compact Object Mass Function — KILLED (Practice)

**Date:** 2026-06-26
**Result:** The mass gap between NS (~2.1 M☉) and BH (~3-5 M☉) is already well-documented in the literature. Combining GW + radio pulsar + X-ray binary masses with irreducibly different selection effects cannot distinguish singular from non-singular scenarios.

## Crap-or-Worthwhile Assessment
**Question:** Does this change what someone would assume or do?

**No.** The "mass gap" is already known and debated. Combining heterogeneous datasets with different selection functions (GW detector sensitivity, radio pulsar beaming, X-ray binary accretion bias) adds confusion to a problem the field has already characterized. The singularity question is philosophically interesting but empirically indistinguishable with mass distribution data alone — both GR and alternatives predict similar mass functions near the TOV limit.

Even if a feature were found, it would be attributed to selection effects or the known mass gap, not to alternatives to singularities.

**Verdict: PRACTICE.** Killed.
