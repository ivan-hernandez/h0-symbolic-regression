# P17: Pulsar Glitch Size Distribution — Optimal Functional Form via SR

## Hypothesis
Pulsar glitch sizes (Δν/ν) follow either:
- **(A) Confirmed** — a known distribution (power law, lognormal) → practice
- **(B) Rejected** — a different form discovered by SR → novel

## Background
Pulsars occasionally undergo sudden spin-up events called glitches. The fractional size Δν/ν spans ~5 dex (10⁻¹¹ to 10⁻⁵). The distribution is debated:
- Power law: P(Δν) ∝ (Δν)^(-α) (Espinoza+2011, α≈1.6-1.8)
- Lognormal: ln(Δν/ν) ~ N(μ,σ) (Melatos+2018)
- Broken power law: different slope above/below threshold
- Pareto: heavy-tailed distribution

No consensus exists. SR can discover the optimal form.
