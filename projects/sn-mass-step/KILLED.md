# SN Host Galaxy Mass Step — KILLED

## Reason: Practice (consistent with literature)

The mass step in Type Ia SN distances at log(M_host/M☉) ≈ 10 with
γ ≈ 0.059 mag is already well-described by a step function. No
hidden complexity to discover.

## Evidence

- **Corrected residual (MU_SH0ES):** γ = 0.014 mag, p = 0.07 —
  marginally significant residual after BBC correction
- **SR on corrected residual:** Best loss 0.02375 (constant) →
  0.02339 (complexity 15). No improvement over constant. SR found
  only noise.
- **Slope in residuals:** High-mass slope −0.005 ± 0.012 mag/dex
  (−0.4σ). Low-mass slope −0.004 ± 0.006 mag/dex (−0.6σ). Neither
  significant.
- **Step width:** Smooth step fit gives c = −0.06 ± 0.27 (0.2σ
  from sharp). Consistent with infinitely sharp step.
- **Redshift evolution:** γ consistent across z ∈ [0.01, 2.5]
  (all p > 0.15). No evolution detected.
- **Optimal threshold:** logM = 11.20 best but t = −1.90 vs
  t = −1.81 at default 10.0. Consistent with 10.0.

## Verdict

The mass step IS a step function. The BBC correction does an
adequate job. SR has nothing to improve. This confirms the
existing literature — practice, not novel.

## Crap-or-Worthwhile

Practice — does not pass the test.
